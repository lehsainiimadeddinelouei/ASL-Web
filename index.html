ABOUT ME

Hello! My name is Lehsaini Imadeddine Louei, but many just call me Imad for short. I was born and raised in Tlemcen, Algeria, a city rich in culture and history. I’ve always had a natural curiosity for how things work, which led me to pursue my studies in Computer Science Engineering at the University of Tlemcen, where I’m currently in my second year.

My journey into technology has always been driven by a desire to create meaningful social impact. One of my proudest achievements is co-developing an AI-powered application that detects hand gestures and converts them into text, designed to support individuals with speech impairments. This project was a collaborative effort involving all five of us: myself, Kerroum Taha, Marhoum Aymen, Lakehal Boumedien, and Aoun Samir. It significantly shaped the way I view the role of technology in promoting accessibility and inclusion.

Smart Glasses with Real-Time ASL Translation Using Raspberry Pi Zero description

These smart glasses translate American Sign Language (ASL) into spoken language using an onboard Raspberry Pi Zero (RPZ), a camera module, mini speakers, and a micro projector. The system is designed for real-time translation with both online and offline capabilities. to create a real-time, interactive communication aid.

Guide

Welcome to our AI-powered American Sign Language (ASL) interpreter tool! This web application allows you to convert ASL hand gestures into written text in real time using your device’s camera. Whether you're just starting to learn sign language or you need a communication aid, this tool is designed to be both intuitive and accessible.

To get started, simply click the 'Get Started' button and grant access to your camera. Once your camera is active, begin signing — our system will analyze your hand movements using machine learning and instantly display the corresponding text on-screen. No downloads or installations are required.

Our mission is to help bridge communication gaps and promote accessibility through cutting-edge technology. Try it now and experience how AI can make sign language interpretation more inclusive and interactive for everyone!
